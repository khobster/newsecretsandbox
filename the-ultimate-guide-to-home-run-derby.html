The History of Home Run Derby Games

The concept of the home run derby originated as a television show in 1960. In this inaugural competition, Mickey Mantle narrowly beat Willie Mays.

Quite the origin story, huh? A couple of New York City baseball legends trading dong knocks over an over for a TV audience that was still new to the groundbreaking medium.

It actually wasn't until 1985 that the game was introduced during the Major League Baseball All-Star festivities. This of course is the version most people are familiar with today.

For the record, and in case anyone asks, the first MLB derby took place at the Hubert Humphrey Metrodome in Minneapolis, Minnesota.

Remember that hefty bag? Kirby Puckett and the rest.
