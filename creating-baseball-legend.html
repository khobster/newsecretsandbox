Creating Your Own Baseball Legend: Customization in Smack Homers

In the bright, starry-eyed universe of online sports RPGs, few are as gratifying as creating and personalizing your own player. Your own avatar. Your person. Or your tomato. Your panda.

This site, smackhomers.com, a leading personalized baseball game, offers the simple ability to craft your baseball or softball character from scratch. This indubitably enhances the gaming experience and makes for an immersive time. Despite the fact that it's just a super dumbed-down video game.

And when we say dumbed down, we mean it.

It's not souped up. It's not 3D. It's stupid graphics that you remember from a time long ago.

Listen, you don't get a lot of options, but you get a few options depending on what side of the plate you swing from. There's the tomato. There's the panda. But then there's your name. Want to play as yourself? Fill it in.

Want to play as Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart? Well go ahead. Be our guest.

Want to play as the candlestick maker? Play as the candlestick maker.

Look, your player will already get a custom shiny metal bat. It stings if you hit it in the cold, but that's like all bats, isn't it? You get colorful gloves, a vibrant uniform, what else do you want? What else do you need?

You're born to smack homers. Dongs over the fence. That's it. It's that simple.

But customization in Smack Homers is not just about aesthetics. This RPG element adds a layer of strategy to the game, as you must decide when is best to swing. And how fast? How quickly?

You are constantly working towards improving your player and achieving new milestones. And by that, we mean upping your numbers. Upping your yahtzee count.
