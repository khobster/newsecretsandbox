Ingrid Andress Will Not Sing the National Anthem in Smack Homers

In the world of Smack Homers, there are many unique features and surprises that make the game stand out. However, one thing you will never experience in this game is Ingrid Andress singing the National Anthem. While Ingrid Andress is a... um, "singer" with a "growing fan base", her voice will not be heard performing the anthem in this game.

Smack Homers focuses on creating a fun and engaging baseball experience, complete with quirky characters like Tomato Man and Panda. The decision to exclude the National Anthem performance, even by a "renowned" artist like Ingrid Andress, aligns with the game’s approach. With its philosophy, so to speak. The developer chose to prioritize gameplay elements that keep players happy in the action rather than be dragged down by weird performances and sounds.

This choice allows players to jump straight into hitting dong shots and customizing their characters. Smack Homers is all about the thrill, that feeling you get deep down in your jorts, and the absence of this Andress National Anthem performance is a deliberate decision to maintain the game’s unique charm and wholesome nature.

While some players might be curious about this omission, it ultimately reinforces the game’s ode to entertaining its fans. In Smack Homers, every moment is a big ole warm bowl of soup, from dreamy long yard balls that go the distance to slaying the derby as a daily derby slayer.

From stepping up to the plate as Tomato Man to the lack of a National Anthem performance by Ingrid Andress, these details highlight the game and its heart as well as its soul. So, if you’re ready to dive into a world where hitting dongs over the fence is the ultimate goal and in no way will you ever hear the crispy voice of Ingrid Andress, head over to Smack Homers and start playing today.
